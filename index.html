History

of Qeparo & Suga Region

Qeparo is a historic village on the Albanian Riviera, divided into Old Qeparo, nestled on a hillside, and New Qeparo, along the Ionian Sea. With roots in ancient Illyrian and Greek civilizations, the village has been shaped by the Romans, Byzantines, and Ottomans, making it a fascinating destination. Today, Qeparo is known for its olive groves, traditional stone architecture, and beautiful seaside.

Guest House Suga, located on a cliffside in the village, offers panoramic views of the Ionian Sea. Once a lookout point to protect against pirate attacks during the Ottoman period, it remains a peaceful spot, perfect for those seeking both tranquility and history.

At Suga, visitors can immerse themselves in Qeparo’s natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. The guest house provides easy access to both the village's historical sites and the breathtaking landscapes of the Albanian Riviera.
